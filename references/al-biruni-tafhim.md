# Al-Biruni — Kitab al-Tafhim (Book of Instruction)

## Identity

- **Full Name:** Abū Rayḥān Muḥammad ibn Aḥmad al-Bīrūnī
- **Lived:** 973–1048 CE (Ghaznavid Empire, modern-day Uzbekistan/Afghanistan)
- **Known as:** Supreme polymath — astronomer, mathematician, historian, astrologer
- **Key Work:** *Kitāb al-Tafhīm li-Awāʾil Ṣināʿat al-Tanjīm* (Book of Instruction in the Elements of the Art of Astrology)

## The Tafhim — Structure

Al-Biruni's *Kitab al-Tafhim* is the clearest, most systematic introduction to traditional astrology ever written. It is structured as 530 questions and answers, divided into 5 main parts:

### Part 1: Geometry and Arithmetic (Questions 1–100)
- Foundations of geometry needed for astrology
- Arithmetic: fractions, sexagesimal system (base-60)
- Spherical geometry and celestial coordinates

### Part 2: Astronomy (Questions 101–250)
- The celestial sphere, circles, and axes
- The zodiac and its division into signs
- The planets: motions, stations, retrogradation
- The Sun and Moon: eclipses, phases
- The fixed stars: magnitude, position
- Time: years, months, days, hours (planetary hours)

### Part 3: Geography and Chronology (Questions 251–310)
- The 7 climates and their characteristics
- Calculating local time from celestial observations
- Calendars: Hijri, Persian, Greek, Coptic

### Part 4: Astrology Proper (Questions 311–470)
- **Signs:** Their natures, elements, qualities, triplicities
- **Planets:** Natures, dignities, debilities, rulerships
- **Houses:** 12 houses and their meanings
- **Lots:** Part of Fortune, Part of Spirit, etc.
- **Aspects:** Conjunction, sextile, square, trine, opposition
- **Strength:** Essential and accidental dignity
- **Nativities:** How to judge a birth chart
- **Transits and revolutions:** Progressions, solar returns

### Part 5: Elections and Interrogations (Questions 471–530)
- Choosing favorable times for actions
- Answering specific questions
- Astrological medicine and critical days

## Core Teachings

### On the Planets (from Tafhim)

| Planet | Nature | Gender | Temper | Color |
|--------|--------|--------|--------|-------|
| Saturn | Cold, dry | Masculine | Melancholic | Black, dark |
| Jupiter | Hot, moist | Masculine | Sanguine | White, green |
| Mars | Hot, dry | Masculine | Choleric | Red |
| Sun | Hot, dry | Masculine | Choleric | Gold |
| Venus | Cold, moist | Feminine | Phlegmatic | White, mixed |
| Mercury | Variable | Both | Variable | Mixed |
| Moon | Cold, moist | Feminine | Phlegmatic | White, silver |

### On Dignity and Debility
Al-Biruni presents the classical system:
- **Domicile:** Planet in its own sign (full dignity)
- **Exaltation:** Planet at its most powerful degree
- **Triplicity:** Day/night rulers of the element group
- **Term (Hadd):** Egyptian bounds within each sign
- **Face (Decan):** 10° divisions
- **Detriment:** Opposite sign from domicile
- **Fall:** Opposite sign from exaltation

### On Aspects
The five Ptolemaic aspects plus conjunction:
- **Conjunction (0°):** Complete blending of planetary energies
- **Sextile (60°):** Harmony and opportunity
- **Square (90°):** Tension and conflict
- **Trine (120°):** Full harmony and flow
- **Opposition (180°):** Polarity and awareness

| House | Name | Meaning |
|-------|------|---------|
| 1 | Ascendant | Life, body, character |
| 2 | Epikrateia | Wealth, resources, gain/loss |
| 3 | Brothers | Siblings, travel, messengers |

| 6 | Health | Illness, servants, weakness |
| 7 | Marriage | Spouse, partnership, open enemies |
| 8 | Death | Mortality, fear, estates |
| 9 | Travel | Long journeys, religion, wisdom |
| 10 | Action (MC) | Career, honor, authority |
| 11 | Good Fortune | Friends, hopes, allies |
| 12 | Evil Spirit | Enemies, imprisonment, suffering |

### On the Lots
- **Part of Fortune:** ASC + ☽ - ☉ (by day), ASC + ☉ - ☽ (by night)
- **Part of Spirit:** ASC + ☉ - ☽ (by day), ASC + ☽ - ☉ (by night)
- **Part of Marriage:** ☾ + cusp 7 - ♀ (for men)
- **Part of Father:** ☉ - ♄ + ASC
- **Part of Mother:** ♀ - ☉ + ASC

### On Elections
General principles from the Tafhim:
- The Moon must be well placed (not void, not combust, not afflicted)
- The ascendant and its lord must be strong
- The planet ruling the matter must be dignified
- Avoid infortunes (Saturn, Mars) in angles
- Consider the planetary hour and day

## Historical Significance
- One of the earliest advocates of empirical observation in astrology
- His clear, systematic presentation made astrology accessible to students
- The *Tafhim* remained a standard textbook for centuries
- Al-Biruni was among the first to question certain astrological dogmas, setting a precedent for critical thinking in astrological practice
