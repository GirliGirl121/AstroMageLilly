## Part 2: The Magic of Light — Philosophy and Foundations

### Definition
> "The spiritual art and science of a light being dwelling in the Light of all Lights."

- Rooted in **union between heart and mind**, nature with spirit, and Gnostic experience of the Divine
- Practical for earthly spells AND sublime spiritual development
- Previously required pilgrimage to North Africa, Turkey, Middle East for initiation

### Core Concepts

#### Rouhaniah (روحانية)
- Spiritual emanations/powers embodied in all living things
- Activated by the Divine Spirit
- Called **Rouhaniah** by Arab magicians, **Pneuma** by Greeks, **Prana** by Hindus
- The power of the mind/imagination is the *medium*, not the *source*
- **Divine Spirit** → influences **soul** → influences **mind** → influences **physical form**

#### The Emerald Tablet (Jabir Ibn Hayyan's translation)
> "That which is above is from that which is below, and that which is below is from that which is above, working the miracles of one, as all things were from one."

#### Cosmology
- The cosmos is a **living entity**, interconnected on all levels (physical, mental, spiritual)
- Everything has a **rouhaniah** that connects it to the One Divine source
- Magic is a path of **wisdom, love, and being**
- Emphasizes love as a **cosmic force** connecting humans with celestial agents

### Ethics
- "Like attracts like" — principle
- The more attuned with **love, light, and life**, the more magic manifests in one's life
- Avoid extremes; magic should be **wilful and joyous**
- Measure magical development by:
  1. How much **Light** is brought into one's life and others
  2. How much **love** is generated from the work

### Magical Worldview
- Traditional magic focused on **spirituality and spirits**
- Spirits were as objectively real to ancient magicians as the Sun and Moon
- Ancient spells call on **angels and spiritual beings** by ancient holy names
- The magician stands not by personal power but by **authority of the Divine**

## Part 3: Historical Lineage and Sources

### Sabians of Harran
- Built **seven planetary temples** to various divinities
- Astrology central to their faith
- Preserved ancient Hermetic knowledge after the fall of pagan temples
- Blended Chaldean, Greek, and Persian traditions

### Tahateel Permutation Method
Each Tahateel name is placed in a 7×7 magic square. Each subsequent line is permuted:
- 5th, 6th, 7th letters → 1st, 2nd, 3rd of next line
- Remaining 4 letters → last 4 of next line
- Continue for 6 lines (7 total including original)

### Four Types of Derived Tahateel Names (per planet)
1. **Inner Power / Potencies** — rows read right-to-left (call forth planetary rouhaniah)
2. **Astrological Names** — rows read left-to-right (call down planetary rays; consecrate talismans)
3. **Names of Charging/Commanding** — columns bottom-to-top (compel spirits)
4. **Names of Burning** — columns top-to-bottom (charge/compel disobedient spirits; thwart adversaries)

## Part 9: The Guardian Angel (Holy Guardian Angel / HGA)

### Angelic Communication Methods
- Through **numerical calculation** (name-based)
- Through **meditation and visionary work**

- Based on birth name (English or original language)

- The resulting number corresponds to an angelic name

- Physical representation of the angel's name and seal
- Worn during rituals

### The Magical Robe
- Consecrated garment for ritual work
- Color corresponds to the planet/day of the work
- Should be kept only for magical use
- Represents the **Garment of Light** physically

### The Angelic Covenant
- Formal pact between the magician and their Guardian Angel
- Establishes a working relationship for life
- Includes:
  - Invocation of the angel
  - A written covenant (signed)
  - Regular devotional practice
  - Commitment to spiritual development
- The angel becomes a constant guide and protector

### The Magus Gates
- **Purpose:** Opening portals between worlds for evocation
- **Design:** Black background, white pentagram (72° angles), golden mean spiral, Ouroboros dragon
- **Babylonian Deities in the Ouroboros** (descending order from tail to head):
  1. Anu (ruler of stars, 1st heaven)
  2. Enlil
  3. Ea
  4. Sin
  5. Shamash
  6. Adad
  7. Ishtar (Supreme Creator, highest heaven)
- Written in **cuneiform** within the dragon's body
- Four portals can be made (one per direction), but most workings need only one
- **Two portals opened simultaneously:** one between our world and spirit realm, another from spirit realm to intended world

- **Order of working:** Start with dominant element in your name; then add friendly element (Fire↔Air, Earth↔Water); then missing elements

## Part 12: Magic of the Jinn

### Core Philosophy
- Jinn magic is an **amazing art** but not the **defining element** of the craft
- Ancient magicians summoned **1-2** jinn who became **brothers/sisters/companions**
- Capable magicians might expand to a **dozen** — beyond that is "playing with fire"
- A **pact** is essential: equal exchange of friendship, NOT selling one's soul
- Summoning without a pact = free-for-all relationship with no obligation

### The Summoner's Way
**80% protection, 20% actual summoning**

#### Three Pieces of Advice
1. **Don't expect to see jinn physically** — they are invisible by nature. You will feel them, see evidence of their action, but rarely see them as physical objects.
2. **Approach them as intelligent beings** — avoid wielding weapons (seen as aggression), don't be hostile unless they are, don't try to bind them (you can't), ensure they leave on friendly terms.
3. **Know the signs of jinn** — know the difference between jinn presence and other spiritual experiences.

### Protection

#### 1. Warding Amulet
Written on thin paper:
- Letter **Qaf** (Arabic) 100× + **Nun** upside-down 50×
- **Great Name of the Elements** 111×
- **Tahateel names** 7× each
- **Berhatiah names** 3× each
- **Seven Angels** and **Four Archangels** 70× each
- **Four Elders** 12× each
- Followed by the **Charge** (protective address to hostile jinn)
- Burn incense, recite all names and the charge 3-7 times
- Wear around neck (except in unclean places)
- Recite the charge at least once a month

#### 2. Shield of the Magi Prayer (recited 7× before conjuration)
(Full text as above in Essential Rites)

#### 3. Release of the Residents
- Works on jinn inhabiting the working area (under King **Taresh**)
- Burn pure frankincense resin and recite 7×
- Names: **Taresh, Maresh, Mareesh, Mareeosh, Baresh, Radash, Latmash, Batash**
- Invokes the seven planetary angels to compel evacuation

#### 4. For Stubborn Jinn (Al-Buni's method)
Write on clean paper: **Haleesha Maleesha Malahah** (Arabic)
Hang on 4 walls; say aloud:
> "Yastates Mates Alehates Yastates Mates Sahates Yates Satates Yemates Ma'aates Seltates Semates Ma'ates; get out, O uninvited jinn, from this place ~ Warkeekum Bakom Kakom Bakom Rass."

- **7 concentric circles** within an outer square with 4 gates
- Drawn with **steel** (spear with steel tip)
- Mixture of dirt, ashes, and salt sprinkled first
- All circles drawn **clockwise**
- Inner circle must be wide enough for the magician's body to lie within
- Begin with outer square, move inward
- **3 gates sealed**, 1 gate left open (direction from which jinn enter)
- Inscriptions from scriptures on each circle
- Steel pieces impaled at each circle's start/end point; each facing a different direction

## Part 13: The Magical Adept

### Two Categories of Adept
1. **Functional Adept** — high proficiency in a given magical system; may be a political title within an order
2. **Spiritual Adept** — very high spiritual development; magical/spiritual master

### Characteristics of a True Adept
- Spiritually labors when others play
- Loves when others hate
- Shows compassion when others judge
- Sees life as transitory
- Cares for the world for future generations
- Plants fruits of goodness; tender words flow from their mouth

### The 3-Fold Sign of a Magical Adept
1. Impetus is **from the Divine**
2. Relies on the **Divine** for all spiritual and magical acts
3. Aspires toward the **Divine reality** above all things

### The Five Components of Adepthood
1. **Footstool** — the path/system followed to reach knowledge of the Divine
2. **Placement** — effort toward transformation through Light (years of spiritual labor, illumination rituals, righteous acts)
3. **State** — what the Divine bestows (personal spiritual nature = individual power)
4. **Station** — revelation of achievement and hidden things (Divine reveals placement after many years)
5. **Mystery** — hidden mysteries of Light revealed by the Holy One and spiritual messengers

## Part 14: Magical Groups

### Cautions
- Beware of excessive focus on **lineage, charters, and trademarks**
- Groups that exalt themselves as **sacred or holy** — potential cult dynamics
- Claims of being **solely contacted by secret masters** — sign of delusion
- **Personality worship** of founder (alive or dead)
- Multiple **vows of secrecy** and excessive hierarchy

### Signs of a Good Group
- Based on **service, dedication, volunteerism, cooperation**
- You feel **loved and supported** even when going against group norms
- Qualified individuals can freely become guides/teachers without interference
- Provides services without needing to prove lineage

### The Magic Society of the White Flame
- The authors' own group
- Approach: nurture love, not politics
- A "tight family of magicians" pursuing similar spiritual aspirations
