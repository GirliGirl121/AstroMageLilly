# Abu Ma'shar al-Balkhi (Albumasar) — Complete System

## Identity

- **Full Name:** Abū Maʿshar Jaʿfar ibn Muḥammad ibn ʿUmar al-Balkhī
- **Latinized:** Albumasar
- **Lived:** 787–886 CE, Abbasid Baghdad
- **Tradition:** Persian-Islamic astrology, synthesis of Greek, Persian, Indian, and Babylonian traditions
- **Known as:** The greatest astrologer of the Abbasid court

## Major Works

### Kitāb al-Madkhal al-Kabīr (The Great Introduction to Astrology)
The most important astrological textbook of the medieval world. It became the standard reference across the Islamic world and later Europe through Latin translations.

**Contents:**
- **Book 1:** Proofs of astrology, astronomical foundations, the zodiac, planets, and their natures
- **Book 2:** The 12 houses, their meanings, and the lot (Part) of Fortune
- **Book 3:** Significations of the planets in the 12 houses
- **Book 4:** Aspects, planetary strength, and the calculation of the lots
- **Book 5:** Revolutions of the years of the world (mundane astrology), conjunctions, and eclipses
- **Book 6:** Astrological history — great conjunctions, religions, and dynasties
- **Book 7:** Nativities and the length of life
- **Book 8:** Elections and interrogations

### Kitāb al-Milal wa al-Duwal (Book of Religions and Dynasties)
A world-historical astrology text predicting the rise and fall of religions and empires based on the great conjunctions of Jupiter and Saturn.

### Kitāb al-Ulūf (Book of Thousands)
On the astrological cycles of history based on the Firdaria and other period systems.

### Kitāb al-Mawālīd (Book of Nativities)
On birth charts and their interpretation — used by later astrologers including Al-Biruni.

### Kitāb al-Ikhtiyārāt (Book of Elections)
On electional astrology — choosing the right time for actions.

## Core Teachings

### The Great Conjunctions Theory
Abu Ma'shar's most original contribution: the 20-year conjunctions of Jupiter and Saturn mark major historical cycles.

| Conjunction Cycle | Meaning |
|-------------------|---------|
| 20-year (Jupiter-Saturn) | Generational change, rise/fall of rulers |
| 240-year (Trigonal shift) | Change of ruling dynasties and religions |
| 960-year (Full cycle) | Complete transformation of world order |

### The Seven Climates (Iqlīm)
Division of the world into 7 astrological climate zones, each ruled by a planet and determining the character of peoples living there.

### Planetary Natures
- **Sun:** Hot and dry, masculine, diurnal — kingly authority
- **Moon:** Cold and moist, feminine, nocturnal — growth and decay
- **Mercury:** Variable, participates in the nature of whatever planet it aspects
- **Venus:** Hot and moist, feminine — love, beauty, harmony
- **Mars:** Hot and dry, masculine — war, conflict, energy
- **Jupiter:** Hot and moist, masculine — wisdom, expansion, justice
- **Saturn:** Cold and dry, masculine — restriction, time, karma

### House System (12 Houses)
1. **Ascendant (Self):** Life, body, character, beginning of things
2. **Epikrateia (Wealth):** Resources, gain, possessions
3. **Brothers:** Siblings, short journeys, communication
4. **Parents:** Father, home, family, land, inheritance
5. **Children:** Offspring, pleasure, creativity, messengers
6. **Health:** Illness, servants, small animals
7. **Marriage:** Partnership, spouse, open enemies, contracts
8. **Death:** Mortality, inheritance, fear, transformation
9. **Travel:** Long journeys, religion, wisdom, dreams
10. **Action (MC):** Career, honor, authority, mother
11. **Good Fortune:** Friends, hopes, benefits, allies
12. **Evil Spirit:** Enemies, imprisonment, suffering, self-undoing

### Lots (Parts)
Abu Ma'shar refined the system of astrological lots:
- **Part of Fortune:** Asc + Moon - Sun — worldly luck and body
- **Part of Spirit:** Asc + Sun - Moon — spirit and inner purpose
- **Part of Marriage:** Asc + Venus - Saturn (for men), Asc + Saturn - Venus (for women)
- **Part of Death:** Asc + 8th cusp - Moon

### On the Strength of Planets
A planet's strength depends on:
1. **Essential dignity:** Sign placement (domicile, exaltation, triplicity, term, face)
2. **Accidental dignity:** House placement (angular, succedent, cadent), speed, direction
3. **Aspectual strength:** Application to benefics vs malefics
4. **Phase:** Visibility, combust, under the Sun's beams

## Influence
- Translated into Latin in the 12th century by John of Seville and Herman of Carinthia
- Became the foundational text of European medieval astrology
- Quoted by Albertus Magnus, Roger Bacon, Dante, and countless Renaissance astrologers
- The standard reference for astrologers from the 12th to the 17th century
