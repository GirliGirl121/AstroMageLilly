# Ahmad al-Buni (أحمد البوني) — Complete System & Teachings
## Compiled from grokipedia, Wikipedia, academia papers, and cross-references

### Identity
- **Full Name:** Abū l-ʿAbbās Aḥmad ibn ʿAlī ibn Yūsuf al-Qurašī al-Būnī
- **Born:** Būna, Almohad Caliphate (modern Annaba, Algeria)
- **Died:** c. 622/1225 CE (under Ayyubids in Cairo)
- **Descent:** Claimed descent from the Quraysh tribe
- **Titles:** Sharaf al-Din, Shihab al-Din, Muhyi al-Din
- **Sufi Lineage:** Trained under Abū Muḥammad ʿAbd al-ʿAzīz b. Abī Bakr al-Mahdawī (who also taught Ibn Arabi)
- **Influences:** al-Ghazālī, Ibn Barrajān, Ibn Masarra, Neoplatonism, Hermetic traditions
- **Primary Fields:** *ʿIlm al-ḥurūf* (science of letters), theurgy, talismans, magic squares, Sufi spirituality

### Key Distinction: Al-Buni's Magic vs. Sihr (Sorcery)
Al-Buni strictly distinguished his practice from illicit sorcery (*siḥr*):
- **Sihr** = Forbidden, harmful, seeking domination over creation
- **Qur'anic Theurgy** = Permitted, grounded in Divine Names and scripture
- **Natural Magic** = Operative, based on inherent properties of elements, letters, and celestial bodies
- His practice was called *'Ilm al-Hikmah* (Knowledge of Wisdom), *'Ilm al-simiyah* (Study of Divine Names), *Ruhaniyat* (Spirituality)

### Major Works
1. ***Shams al-Maʿārif wa-Laṭāʾif al-ʿAwārif*** — The Sun of Gnosis. His foundational work.
   - **NOTE:** The expanded *Shams al-Maʿārif al-Kubrā* is a pseudepigraphic 11th/17th-century compilation, NOT by al-Buni
   - Contains 40+ unnumbered chapters (*fasl*)
   - Over 40 distinct occult practices tied to the 99 Names of God
   - Famous ritual: *Birhatiya* (Ancient Oath / Red Sulphur)
2. ***Laṭāʾif al-Ishārāt fī l-Ḥurūf al-ʿUlwiyyāt*** — Foundational lettrist text
3. ***Manbaʿ Uṣūl al-Ḥikmah*** — The Source of the Essentials of Wisdom
4. ***al-Lumʿa al-Nūrānīyah fī Awrād al-Rabbānīyah*** — Invocatory prayers & talismanic cosmology
5. ***Hidāyat al-Qāṣidīn wa-Nihāyat al-Wāṣilīn*** — Sufi paths integrated with esoteric practices
6. ***Sharḥ Ism Allāh al-aʿẓam fī al-rūḥānī*** — Commentary on the Greatest Name of God
7. ***Treatise on the 99 Names of God*** (Khalili Collection MSS 300)
8. ***Berhatiah*** — Ancient Magick Conjuration of Power (attributed)

### The Science of Letters (*ʿIlm al-Ḥurūf*)

#### Core Doctrine
- The **28 Arabic letters** are the primordial building blocks of creation
- They are infixed into the human intellect, spirit, and body
- Each letter has a **numerical value** (abjad system), a **celestial correspondence**, a **service angel**, and specific powers

#### Letter Correspondences
- Each letter has:
  - A **spirit** (*ruhaniyya*) — an angelic regent who serves the letter
  - A **celestial body** (planet or fixed star) associated with it
  - An **element** (fire, air, water, earth) inherited from the four elements
  - A **zodiacal correspondence** (through the 28 lunar mansions)
  - **Therapeutic properties** (healing, protection, attraction)
  
- **Example:** The letter *Ra (ر)*, value 200
  - Angel: *Dehiryail/Dahariel* — angel of the Fifth Heaven
  - Function: Charged with maintenance of existing things
  - Power: Can increase yield of fruit trees, assist the caller in any endeavor
  - Element: Air (Mercurial)

#### Three Operations on Letters
1. **Tafṣīl** (تفسيل) — Analysis/articulation: Breaking a word into its constituent letters
2. **Taksīr** (تكسير) — Fracture: Breaking a number into its digit sum or component values
3. **Basṭ** (بسط) — Expansion: Writing a word in its full spelled-out letter names (e.g., "Allah" → "Alif Lam Lam Ha")

### Magic Squares (*Awfaq* / أوفاق)

A cornerstone of al-Buni's esoteric framework — viewed as microcosmic representations of celestial order.

#### Construction Method
- Al-Buni showed how to construct magic squares using a **simple bordering technique** (c. 1200 CE)
- He constructed **4×4 Latin squares** using letters from the 99 Names of God
- Each square is associated with specific:
  - **Planetary seal** (pattern connecting every cell)
  - **Angelic hierarchy** (named spirits to invoke)
  - **Timing** (planetary hour/day for construction/activation)
  - **Materials** (metal, stone, ink color, incense)

### The 99 Names of God (*Asma' al-Husna*)

Al-Buni's system is deeply interwoven with the 99 Names:
- Each name has a **numerical value** (sum of its letters' abjad values)
- Each name corresponds to a **planet**, **element**, and **sphere of influence**
- Names are used in:
  - **Talismans** — inscribed on materials for specific purposes
  - **Dhikr** — rhythmic recitation for spiritual states
  - **Magic squares** — placed in cells according to numerical value
  - **Invocation** — calling upon the divine attribute through the name

- Use: Breaking opposition, subduing enemies, cutting harmful bonds
- Element: Fire
- Planet: Mars

### Talismanic Practice

#### Talisman Components
1. **Material** — Metal, stone, paper, or clay; varies by planetary nature
2. **Inscriptions** — Divine Names, Qur'anic ayat, letter combinations, magic square
3. **Timing** — Elected astrological moment (planetary hour + mansion + phase)
4. **Activation Ritual** — Invocation, fumigation (*bukhur*), prayer
5. **Condition** — Ritual purity (*taharah*), specific clothing, direction (qibla)

#### Conditions for Talismanic Work
- **Ritual purity** (*ṭahāra*) — essential before any operation
- **Intention** (*Niyyah*) — the practitioner's intent is paramount
- **Secrecy** — knowledge requires supervision by a qualified Sufi master
- **Alignment** — the work must align with divine will, not personal whim

> Key Quote: *"If the secrets of God the Highest were made plain to the common people it would be the cause of their disunion and destruction."*

#### The Birhatiya (The Ancient Oath)
- Famous ritual from *Shams al-Ma'arif*
- A powerful conjuration invoking the highest divine names
- Used for compelling spirits and jinn to service
- Requires extensive preparation and purification
- Considered dangerous for the unprepared

### Lunar Mansion System (*Manazil al-Qamar*)

Al-Buni follows the 28-mansion system with specific letter and divine name assignments for each:
- Each mansion has an **Arabic name** and **meaning**
- Each has a **temperament** (hot/cold/dry/moist)
- Each has specific **operations** suited to its nature
- **Spirit regents** govern each mansion (both angelic and jinn)

### Spiritual Ascent (*Mi'raj*)

Al-Buni describes a path of spiritual ascent through:
1. **Khalwa** (seclusion) — 40-day retreats
2. **Fasting** — purification of the body
3. **Dhikr** — rhythmic invocation of divine names (especially "Allah")
4. **Contemplation of letters** — meditating on the forms and sounds
5. **Dream incubation** (*istikhara*) — seeking guidance through visions
6. **Achieving *kashf*** (unveiling) — direct spiritual perception

### Practical Application Framework

#### For Daily Life:
1. **Protection talismans** — Ayat al-Kursi + appropriate magic square
2. **Healing** — Specific surahs (Al-Fatihah, Al-Shifa ayat) + planetary correspondences
3. **Prosperity** — Jupiter square + Surah al-Waqi'ah recitation
4. **Love** — Venus square + Surah al-Rum 30:21
5. **Wisdom/Knowledge** — Mercury square + Divine Names al-'Alim, al-Hakim

#### For Spiritual Development:
1. **Daily dhikr** of the 99 Names according to planetary day
2. **Letter meditation** — contemplating the form and sound of specific letters
3. **Magic square construction** as a meditative practice
4. **Lunar mansion awareness** — aligning activities with the Moon's station

### Influence & Legacy
- **Hurufism** — Directly influenced the Hurufi movement
- **Bábism** — Indirectly influenced through talismanic letter practices
- **Yoruba Muslim healers** — His works remain a reference in Nigeria
- **Modern Western occult** — Influenced through translations by Nineveh Shadrach and others
- **Controversy** — Banned in Saudi Arabia, criticized by Ibn Taymiyya
- **Academic study** — Major research by Jean-Charles Coulon, Saiyad Nizamuddin Ahmad
